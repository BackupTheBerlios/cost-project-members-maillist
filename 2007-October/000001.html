Dear all,

I have a _fundamental _question about the structure of the Data Exchange 
Format, and more particularly about the CS-CA table.

The label of this table is
Sex-Maturity-Age-Weight-Length record (CA) in commercial samplings data (CS)

The SGRN-06-03 agreed to split the collection of data in two families, 
namely the fishery based and the population based data. The population 
based data, given to be independent from any gear type or fishing 
activity, is precisely the maturity-at-age, weight-at-age and 
age-at-length.

The linkage between the catches, related to fishing activities, and the 
age, related to the population, is done by the length structure, the 
time period and the area. For more information on this, please read the 
SGRN-06-03 report before arguing on it in this forum.

Pragmatically for the DEF, my suggestion (to be discussed) would be to
    1 - unlink CA from CS
    2 - add the fields Date or Quarter and Month
    3 - modify Trip number, station no, Catch category and landing 
category to optional (there can be a direct link for e.g. deriving 
survey indices, subsampling for age directly from length sampling, ...)
   
I see that in the acoustic data there is a CA table also, but with 
different fields. To my mind we should set up a standalone CA with all 
the fields we need (those in CS.CA are Ok) rather than linking one to 
the commercial sampling, one to the acoustic sampling and another one 
which would be independent (purchase of fish for SMALK, ...).

Sorry, I should have seen that right from the start, and thanks to Steve 
for his suggestion to me of a possible CA problem.

Best regards
