Dear Teunis,

many thanks for your effort and congratulations for this.
I wish a bright future for this FF-COST Data Exchange Format Specification.

My only consideration today is that you provided us with a pure FF DEFS. 
In order to make it a real FF-COST DEFS, I will remove all the tables 
and appendices not discussed within COST from your files and I will 
extend to the whole world a certain number of fixed list in the appendix 
I. This means that :

    * I remove the 3 tables CC and the 8 tables from Acoustic Surveys
      stage 1 and stage 3.
    * I keep the section on tables TS
    *  From the appendix,
          o I remove the sections project as it is a free field.
          o I modify the reference for Area (too much restrictive to the
            ICES world), we must refer to the list available on the JRC
            website
            http://datacollection.jrc.ec.europa.eu/c/document_library/get_file?folderId=2502&name=DLFE-261.xls
            and on FAO website http://www.fao.org/fishery/area/search
          o I modify the reference for Fishing Gear to refer to ISSCFG
            ftp://ftp.fao.org/FI/DOCUMENT/cwp/handbook/annex/AnnexM1fishinggear.pdf
          o I add the mention that the list of Rectangles only applies
            to ICES areas
          o I add the international species reference list :
            ftp://ftp.fao.org/FI/stat/data/ASFIS_sp.zip
          o I remove all AS codes
          o There are other modalities for maturity scale, such as WKMAT
            (1-5) and IBTS (1-4), do we include them in the reference
            list? or do we leave this list opened. Same for Commercial
            Size category scale and Subsampling category.
          o Do we keep the Fishing activity category National in the
            appendix empty?

_ The modifications suggested do not affect the structure agreed so 
far_, only the presentation and some of the reference lists. You will 
find enclosed my proposal for the joint FF-COST DEFS. This is not meant 
to delay any further the final proposal. I imagine we can have a quick 
exchange on it to fix all possible mistakes and/or ommission.

Later, all discussions on the format occuring while developing the COST 
packages will have to first consider this DEFS as the reference. There 
must be rooms for improving this format and we'll have to get strong 
arguments and reach a full consensus to modify any part of it.

Best regards.

Joel
