>     Dear all,
>
>     prior to the meeting and summarising the latest discussions in the
>     mailing list, in the forum and in multilateral correspondance,
>     here are the issues to be discussed regarding the COST-FF Data
>     Exchange Format :
>
>     * the major discussion is about the sex field in hl table. The
>     issue is about extending this concept to other possibilities than
>     F/M and also extending the concept to take into consideration the
>     taxa in a sampled box.
>     The system today is that a given hh$staNum or hh$trpCode is
>     documented with sl$spp related to cl$spp and ce$spp at the
>     population level. for this sl$spp we may have different sexes
>     (case of Nephrops) registered in hl$sex.
>     The first problem is that we don't have the sampled weight per
>     sex. It's not a problem to estimate the total number and the total
>     length structure per sex, but how do we calculate the total weight
>     per sex?
>     The second point is about burried Nephrops. I must say I don't see
>     where is the problem here. The field is optional for unsexed and,
>     unless I miss something, you cannot sex the burried Nephrops? Do
>     we open this field to other occurences? B(urried), ...
>     The third point is about extending the concept of sex to taxa
>     because for me it is the same issue for raising. Some sl$spp
>     (Anglerfish, squids, ...) are a mixture of different species in
>     the sampled box. The raising must be done at the level of
>     taxon/sex, although we only have spp info in cl and ce. If we
>     extend the concept of sex to a field called taxon which would
>     include the sex, it can be a way out to this. This field would be
>     optional in case of similarity with hl$spp.
>
>     * including survey data in COST, and in particular DATRAS data. We
>     only need this information for stock-related variables (sex-ratio,
>     maturity, individual weight, age-at-length). The principle agreed
>     at the beginning of the project is that the ca table would be
>     attached to tr only , same as for the purchase of fish. In the ca
>     table we made sure we had all the information needed like time
>     strata (quarter, month) and space strata (area, rectangle). I
>     understand that by doing so we do not include the DATRAS data as
>     such in COST but COST is not meant to calculate survey indices.
>     During this project we should stick to this, although there are ca
>     fields that can be used to link ca to hh and/or sl if needed
>     (staNum, sex, ...), but this should not be the rule.
>
>     * some minor issues like
>     - should vessel type (tr) be mandatory or optional. IMO what
>     should be mandatory are the fields essential to the estimation of
>     the variables and their precision. COST is not a database.
>     Following this, vessel type should not be mandatory.
>
>     - should gear (hh) corresponds only to the survey gear and not
>     commercial where there are two metier fields (field 25 and 26).
>     Only one of these fields is mandatory and it is the metier level 5
>     (Gear + Target Assemblage). In the new DCR Regulation, the level 6
>     is mandatory (Gear + Target Assemblage + Mesh size and selective
>     device). Anyhow, it will take time before all the countries are
>     able to document levle 6 for all their fisheries. The situation as
>     it is now is a good compromise, level 5 is mandatory in cs, cl and
>     ce. Implicitly, this means that you have to know the gear to
>     document level 5 for your fisheries. I would change nothing here.
>
>     - length corresponds only to total length. IMO it is not advisable
>     to proceed to transformation of variable prior to use it in COST.
>     I mean the length as it is measured will be used in age_length
>     keys and we would find the same information both in hl and in ca.
>     Tansforming to total length in both ca and hl would lead to errors
>     and increase of uncertainty. The solution would be to add a field
>     Length type to hl and ca to ensure that they are both compatible.
>
>     Probably there are some other issues, so don't hesitate to mention
>     them. You may react to my suggestions, especially the FF (not
>     France Football) players! The outcome of this discussion and the
>     agreements will be summarised in the report of the meeting.

>     Lisa has written some SAS code that converts data from the DATRAS

>     Here are comments from the very recent workshop (WKMOG) that used
>     the data format (and my thoughts)
>
>     Code lists (should be easy):
>
>     The field “otolith side” could include an option for when other
>     calcified structures are used (i.e. illicia, spines…).
>
>     The coding system used for rectangle and/or area for the
>     Mediterranean countries would need to be incorporated (SAC-GFCM
>     Geographical Sub-Areas in the Mediterranean (Rome, 2006)).
>
>     Extra field (not needed immediately, one for the future?)
>
>     The biological samples (CA) table does not take account of
>     histology results, these are necessary if a correction factor or
>     calibration is required for the maturity stages. an extra,
>     optional, field is necessary for the microscopic maturity stage
>     results.
>
>     Field description (easy)
>
>     Due to interpretation issues with the ‘length class’ field and its
>     corresponding field ‘length code’, it was decided that a fuller
>     explanation was required, i.e. In mm, Actual length or lower bound
>     of length class e.g. 650 for 65-66 cm.
>
>     Extra field (already in topics to discuss)
>
>     When survey data are used the species list (SL) table should
>     include a field for sex. This is necessary as some species of fish
>     are sexed prior to weighing, such as flat fish and elasmobranches.
>     This extra field is necessary in order to raise maturity correctly.

> Clarifications on dealing with Nephrops:
>
> Carapace lengths are used to estimate weight, and where only tails are 
> available the width is measured and can be converted to an approximate 
> length, thence weight.
>
> The proportions of tails in a sample is both variable and can be quite 
> high apparently.
>
> The issue seems to me that in terms of deriving precision estimates we 
> need to be considering the precision of the measured variable, rather 
> than a derived measure, therefore we are dealing with the lowest level 
> of disaggregated data available therefore we need to include tail width.
>
> It also seems to me that this is just another sorting process Nephrops 
> have in effect been sorted into tails and wholes all we need to do is 
> include “Nephrops” into $commCatScl (commercial size category scale) 
> in the Sl table with “tail” and “whole” (or “carapace”) being added to 
> the appropriate code list for $commCat
>
> Berried Nephrops (females with eggs) (apologies for the buried/berried 
> confusion) as I understand it have different catchabilities in trawls 
> and pots and form different proportions of the catch at different 
> times of the year. Can this also not be very easily accommodated in 
> the CA table with additions of “Nephrops” to the code list for 
> $matScale (maturity scale) and “berried” and “not berried” in 
> $matSatge (maturity stage)
>
> All the best
