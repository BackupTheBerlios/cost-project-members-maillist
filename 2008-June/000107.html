Dear all,

per definition, size category sampling means that the characteristics 
of the sorting is similar for all the trips, and the sampling unit is a 
size category. Of course, the degree of similarity varies from one trip 
to another, etc... but the most important is that the size grades seen 
on board (CS tables) can be raised to the overall weight of the same 
size grades estimated from the landings statistics (CL table).

When it is not the case (as is the Scottish case), the sampling 
strategy becomes a métier sampling, i.e. the sampling unit is a Fishing 
Operation or a Fishing trip. The size category is a mean to organise 
the sub-sampling and help the sampler to be more precise and accurate. 
In this case, the information estimated from the landings statistics is 
the weight landed for that métier (combination of gear, target species, 
area and time). The information in HL is the number of fish really 
measured, corresponding to SubSample weight in SL. The raising factor 
is SL$Weight/SL$Subsample weight.

Concerning the comment "For market sampling: Catch weight is per 
definition equal to Sample weight.". I understand it's confusing, as 
there is the possibility to subsample in the case of métier sampling. 
Maybe Teunis could give us his thoughts about this. I would think it's 
just a problem of label.

In all cases, summing over the HL$No at length should always give us 
the number of fish sampled.

I also reiterate my demand to get more datasets available from ALL the 
institutes involved in COST. this is becoming URGENT and must be given 
highest priority.
