Hi all,

thanks David for your organisational email. I wish to read the response to
your queries from all the persons you have named in your message (be it
negative, positive, critical or even providing solutions,...).

It's Ok for me to chose COSTdbe for this package

Concerning the implementation, here are some points :
1 - For proportion mature estimates (as for sex-ratio), we need
maturity-at-length and maturity-at-age estimated at the population level.
The M-at-length estimates is straightforward as we can use the information
available in the CA table as such, but it is not always the case for
M-at-age. If the information in the CA is coming from a random sampling of
the population itself (scientific survey) then we can take this for granted
and calculate directly M-at-Age. If not, we need to weight the M-at-Length
we have in the CA by the length structure of the population. This we don't
have, but we have a length structure of the catches (see point 2) which can
be used for the ages fully recruited. My point would be not to try to do
what we cannot do or force the system, at the exception of the assumption
that under a certain L, all individuals are considered Immature.

2 -Mathieu has developed some analytical estimates of the length structure
from market sampling (for Ifremer use) based on the COST objects, so I ask
him to tell the group (especially for Marcel) which functions he already
wrote and provide the code to the group. As a general statement, always make
use of already defined functions in COST, (at least internally within the
package and those defined in COSTcore and COSTeda).

3 - We are not talking here about GLM or any kind of modelling. It is
design-based estimates of the means at length/age and the associated
variances.
